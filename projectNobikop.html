The Mobile Application for Cinema Ticket Booking

Visit Figma

Project overview

Product Explanation

Nobikop is an application for film enthusiasts in Indonesia. With Nobikop, users can purchase tickets from all major cinemas in Indonesia. Additionally, Nobikop also has features for ordering food and beverages. The target age range of Nobikop users is 20 to 40 years old.

Duration

The duration for planning this project is 1 month.

Problem Statement

The challenges include difficulty in ordering food and beverages within the same cinema ticket booking application, limited payment methods, and the inability to download barcodes.

My Role

UX Designer, UX Researcher

User research: Summary

I have conducted a survey among cinema-goers to identify the challenges they encounter when booking tickets online. According to the survey findings, the majority of respondents opt for online ticket booking applications to minimize waiting times. However, a significant issue arises from the inability to order food and beverages within a single application. To do so, users are required to install separate applications based on the cinema chain. For example, patrons must install M-Tix to order refreshments at XXI cinemas, while CGV Cinemas requires a distinct application for the same purpose.

Usability Study Plan : Insight

The moderated usability study aims to assess the ease of the cinema ticket booking and food and beverage ordering process in the application. A total of 10 participants who enjoy watching movies in theaters and use cinema ticket booking applications will take part in this usability study.

Insight

1. It turns out that users want the movie synopsis page and movie schedule to be combined into one.

2. It turns out that users find white color more pleasant to look at than blue color on the seat confirmation pop-up.

Design Modifications

To prevent users from having to navigate back and forth between pages when they want to view the synopsis and schedule, the synopsis menu is displayed alongside the schedule.

Takeaways

Impact:

The application design has successfully facilitated users in booking cinema tickets and ordering food or beverages with an attractive interface.

The lesson I learned:

Here I learned that designing a UI/UX is not just about designing based on what we want and think, but also understanding the users. In the initial stage, research is conducted to empathize with their problems, and in the end, research is also conducted to validate their needs.
